About Me

Yuan Zhang is currently an algorithm engineer at ByteDance AML. Before that, he received his Master degree in Peking University (PKU), under the supervision of Prof. Jian Cao, and received his Bachelor degree from Hohai University.

His major research interests lie within computer vision and model compression, such as

  • Knowledge Distillation (KD)
  • Light Weight Object Detection
  • Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM)

I'm expected to graduate May 2024!

Hello, I'm Evonne! I am a Ph.D. student in UC Berkeley's EECS program, where I am co-advised by Prof. Trevor Darrell and Prof. Angjoo Kanazawa. My current interests revolve around using computer vision techniques to investigate how humans interact in social settings. I am excited about applying this insight towards enabling machines to engage in social interactions.


Prior, I was fortunate to have Prof. Kristen Grauman as my research advisor, during which we worked on ego-centric video analysis for ego-pose prediction. I recieved a B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering and a B.S. in Computer Science from The University of Texas at Austin.
