Biography

About Me

Yuan Zhang is a Ph.D. candidate at Peking University, advised by Prof. Shanghang Zhang and Prof. Kuan Cheng. His research focuses on efficient and scalable multimodal learning, with an emphasis on improving the data efficiency, robustness, and reasoning capabilities of large models.

His work spans multimodal large language models and efficient AI, including knowledge distillation, pruning, and quantization. He has published at top-tier venues such as NeurIPS, ICML, and CVPR.

Previously, he received his M.Eng. degree from Peking University and B.Eng. degree from Hohai University. He has interned at SenseTime Research, Alibaba DAMO Academy, ByteDance Seed, and UC Berkeley BAIR.
